    Making A CheckList

    Overview

    In this section, we will explore another basic use of Excel: Creating a Checklist. A checklist is a list of items that need to be completed or checked off. It is a useful tool for:

    • Tracking the completion of various tasks or items.
    • Organizing and prioritizing tasks efficiently.
    • Visual cue for what's been accomplished and what's pending.
    • Monitoring progress toward goals or deadlines

    In this tutorial, we will be creating a CheckList in the context of budgeting as we did in previous tutorials. We will work on a new worksheet, and keep track of how much was "actually" spent on certain expenses compared to "expected" expense per given date.

    We will also expand on different use of formatting and a new use to it.

    Warning

    In order to do this tutorial, you should have successfully completed the previous tutorials: Creating a budget and Creating a chart

    Setting up

    We will first create a new worksheet and add new labels.

    1. Click on the Plus sign to create a new Sheet, and rename the sheet to “CheckList”.

    2. Hover Over the border between column A and column B, until you see the cursor become a separator.

      • This image displays different columns, but the logic applies.

    3. Click and drag to the right to enlarge the size of column A and its cells below.

      • Enlarging the size of the cells are beneficial as if they are not big enough, data in the cells might not be fully visible. We are enlarging cells in column A as it may contain lengthy text type labels.

    4. Label cells in row 1 from column A to E in sequence: “Task,” “Expected,” “Actual,” “Date,” "Check”:

      • Since we are using budgeting as an example for the entirety of the tutorials, we will use labels, "Expected" and "Actual" from previous tutorials to keep track of the "actual" data compared to "Expected" data.

    Formatting cells under labels

    Info

    Cells can be Formatted even if they are empty. Their input will automatically get converted to their given Format.

    Warning

    Format can only be applied to certain data type of the cell input, so in the case of formatting multiple cells, it is essential that data type of the cells are consistent. For instance, we cannot format a text into currency.

    1. Click and drag the cells under "Expected" and "Actual to needed amount.

      • In our example, we are highlighting the cells under those labels down to row 11. We will be inputting data in these cells to keep track of the budget. Dragging the mouse over the cells will highlight them, we are only highlighting the cells that are going to be used to keep track of the budget.

    2. Format cells Under Labels “Expected” and “Actual” to currency (As previously explained in Creating a budget)

    3. Format the cells under label “date” by going to the drop down menu on the “Number” sub section and set it to “Long Date”

      • Again, data format can be done before inputting data. This will make the input data into date format.

      Info

      The Date input must have three sections divided by slash or dash in order to work, but date usually follows the date format(YYYYMMDD).

    Creating a checklist

    Info

    To create a checklist, we need to access the "Developer" Tab, but we need to manually add it, as it may not be provided in Excel by default.

    1. Right Click The “Review” Tab on the top of the Navigation Bar and hit the “customize Ribbon Option”, and check mark the “Developer” Option, press OK.



    2. Add a checkbox by going to the Developer Tab, click on “Insert” dropdown menu, and click on “CheckBox” icon under “Form Controls”,then Click on the cell under the label “Check” to add the checkbox.

    3. Resize the checkBox to fit in the cell under "Check" cell, a row below, by clicking and dragging one of the circles at one of the edges on the Check Box icon:

    4. Hover the mouse on the bottom right side edge of the Check Box cell until you see a black cross.

      • The image displays "2" but you can imagine it being Check Box instead.

    5. Click and drag down the mouse to row 10 in order to duplicate the check boxes

      • In this example, we extended the cells under Check to row 10 to duplicate the Check Box icons

    6. Fill Up The labels Under “Task”, “Expected”,”Actual”, “Date”, as necessary.

      • This will be a constant update as new task arrive and new data in inputted.

    7. Press Ctrl + S and Save the File.
